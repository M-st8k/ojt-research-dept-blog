19/06/2023

Monday ‚óèWeek 1

  • collapse expand

This day feels so heavy since I woke up at 3 am just to catch a bus from Camarines Sur to Albay. Everything was so sudden since I haven't even finished some of the requirements, but time is of the essence because we need to complete 240 hours. I was assigned to our lovely supervisor, Ms. Mary Jane Burce. We are a team of seven students working under her in the Research Coordinators Office. The first thing we did was have a meeting where she gave us some rundown on the do's and don'ts of office work, providing insights on what we should do and expect while we are here in the department. That was in the morning, and in the afternoon, we helped Sir Ryan finish the documents he was preparing for a visit from CHED albeit it is not a lot since there is a recognition. The first day was fruitful and enjoyable too since this is my first time experiencing what it feels like to work in a department.

20/06/2023

Tuesday ‚óèWeek 1

  • collapse expand

This day is just a continuation of yesterday afternoon's work for Sir Ryan. You may be wondering why we are still working with Sir Ryan when we are assigned to Madam Burce. We are temporarily assigned to help him with the documents he is preparing since there are a lot of us‚Äîseven, to be exact. We are divided into two groups. The first group consists of two girls who are under Ms. Burce. They handle the requirements delivered by students under her supervision in Block B. The rest of us, including me, handle Sir Ryan's document-related tasks. We have been assured that this is only a temporary situation, more like a proxy. We handle all the work flawlessly, and at the end of the day, we receive some news. Tomorrow, there will be a celebration involving the university and we are invited to attend while wearing blue shirts. However, since my wardrobe mainly consists of black, gray and, white, I was forced to buy a new shirt. Deep down, I can't wait for tomorrow's event.

21/06/2023

Wednesday ‚óèWeek 1

  • collapse expand

Today was Bicol University's Charter Day, a celebration similar to a foundation day. The day started with a spirited parade from the University Main campus to Astrodome, despite the scorching heat. It was my first time participating in such an event, and I thoroughly enjoyed the lively atmosphere. The festivities continued with various activities and joyful moments, culminating in a venue filled with free food, including a tempting lasagna. The event was a resounding success, leaving us with happy memories and a sense of unity as we headed home.

22/06/2023

Thursday ‚óèWeek 1

  • collapse expand

On this day, we were assigned to different teams within the department, bidding farewell to two members who transferred elsewhere. Under the guidance of Ms. Burce, we embarked on a challenging task: developing an On-the-Job Training Management System (OMS). As we assisted with document preparations, we also planned and brainstormed ideas for the OMS in parallel. The day was a blend of productivity and anticipation, as we laid the groundwork for a system that would transform how we handle training requirements.

23/06/2023

Friday ‚óèWeek 1

  • collapse expand

As the week came to a close, I reflected on my fulfilling journey during my first week at Bicol University. Today, I focused on installing necessary dependencies for the management system we were developing. Carrying our laptops daily became a routine, allowing us to work on assigned tasks when not required by Madam Burce. Additionally, we began networking activities, fixing and connecting ethernet cables in the laboratory, likely in preparation for an upcoming CHED visitation for accreditation and inspection. On this last day of the week, I took time to identify areas for improvement, recognizing that I still had room to grow.
